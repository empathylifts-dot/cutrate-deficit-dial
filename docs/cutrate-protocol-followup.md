# CutRate Protocol Follow-Up

This is the first-week nurture after someone submits Deficit Dial.

## Immediate Email

Subject:

```text
Your CutRate is set
```

Body:

```text
Hey {{contact.first_name}},

Your Deficit Dial is set.

CutRate: {{contact.cutrate__weekly_rate}} lb/week
Calories: {{contact.deficit_dial__daily_calories}}
Macros: {{contact.deficit_dial__macros}}

Here is the first rule: do not adjust after one weigh-in.

Run the target for 7 days. Weigh in daily if you can, then look at the weekly trend. One high morning does not mean the plan failed. One low morning does not mean you should cut harder.

The whole point of your CutRate is to find the deficit you can repeat without the rebound loop: flat training, hunger spikes, low energy, and the weekend erasing everything.

Day 1 job: hit calories and protein. That is it.
```

## Immediate SMS

## Day 2 Email

Subject:

```text
The first 48 hours are not the trend
```

Body:

```text
Scale jumps in the first couple days are usually noise.

More carbs, sodium, a hard lift, poor sleep, soreness, a later dinner. Any of those can move the scale without meaning fat gain.

Stay on the target. Hit protein. Keep steps normal. Do not try to "make up" for a random weigh-in by slashing calories.
```

## Day 4 Email

Subject:

```text
This is where people usually overcorrect
```

Body:

```text
Around day 4, most people start negotiating.

If weight is down, they want to cut harder.
If weight is flat, they want to cut harder.
If hunger is up, they assume the plan is working and still cut harder.

That is the rebound setup.

Your job is to hold the CutRate long enough to read the trend. If training is already feeling flat, that is data too.
```

## Day 7 Email

Subject:

```text
Now read the trend
```

Body:

```text
Now you have enough signal to make a smarter adjustment.

Do not compare your highest weigh-in to your lowest weigh-in. Compare the average.

If the average is moving near your CutRate, keep going.
If nothing moved and tracking was honest, adjust small.
If you were starving, flat, and already thinking about bailing, the issue is not discipline. The issue is the setup.

This is where Performance Protected Cut starts: keeping the cut moving without turning it into the rebound loop again.
```
